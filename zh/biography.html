Composer & Pianist

Drawing inspiration from literature, philosophy, religion, paintings, science, and technology, Hongwei Cai’s music has always shown a particular fondness for using various forms of sonic representation to convey narrative imagination, with a special focus on themes of love and humanity. He has composed music for a wide range of ensembles, skillfully blending idiomatic writing with unique sound effects to create a distinctive voice in our generation. Through collaborations with both university-level musicians and world-class artists, his compositions showcase a complex conceptualization and practical execution.

Hongwei Cai, a Chinese composer and pianist, is currently a Ph.D. candidate in music composition at the University of Iowa. His works have been performed by the Ensemble Dal Niente, the University of Michigan’s Contemporary Directions Ensemble, the University of Cincinnati College-Conservatory of Music Chamber Orchestra, the University of Iowa Center for New Music, the Ensemble Accroche Note, and the JACK Quartet. His music has been presented at venues including the 2021 Society of Composers Online National Conference, the Midwest Graduate Music Consortium (University of Michigan in 2021), the Midwest Composers’ Symposium (University of Michigan in 2017, University of Cincinnati College-Conservatory of Music in 2018 and University of Indiana in 2019), and the Exchange of Midwest Collegiate Composers (University of Iowa in 2018). Hongwei Cai received a MM degree in composition from Oklahoma City University, and a Bachelor of Literature in composition and theory from Shenyang Conservatory of Music in China.
